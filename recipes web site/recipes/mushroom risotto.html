Risotto:

1 - Warm Broth:
In a small pot, keep the broth on low heat—it needs to be warm to absorb properly.
2 - Sauté base:
In a medium saucepan, heat olive oil/butter over medium. Add onion/shallots and cook until soft (~3–4 min). Add garlic and mushrooms; cook until mushrooms are browned and softened (~5–6 min). Salt lightly.
3 - Toast Rice:
Add arborio rice to the pan and stir to coat in the oils. Cook 1–2 minutes until it smells slightly nutty.
4 - Deglaze with wine (if using):
Pour in the wine and stir until absorbed.
5 - Build the risotto:
Add warm broth 1 ladle at a time, stirring often. Wait until the liquid is absorbed before adding more. Continue this process for ~18–22 minutes, or until rice is al dente and creamy.
6 - Finish it:
Turn off heat. Stir in Parmesan and 1–2 tbsp butter. Season with salt & pepper. Optional: fresh parsley or thyme, lemon zest, drizzle of truffle oil.
7 - Serve hot:
You can top it with extra parm or sautéed mushrooms for flair.

Chicken Thighs (Optional):

1 - Prep Chicken:
Pat thighs dry with paper towel—moisture prevents crisping. Season generously with salt, pepper, and spices/herbs of choice.
2 - Sear:
Heat olive oil in a skillet over medium-high until shimmering. Place chicken skin-side down (or top side for boneless). Don't move it—let it sear 6–8 minutes until golden and crispy.
3 - Flip & finish:
Flip and lower heat to medium. Cook another 6–10 min, depending on thickness, until cooked through (internal temp should hit 165°F / 74°C).
4 - Optional butter baste:
Add a knob of butter + fresh thyme/rosemary and spoon melted butter over the chicken for 1–2
5 - Rest:
Let rest 5 minutes off heat. Slice if boneless, or serve whole.
6 - Serve with risotto:
Plate chicken right on top or alongside the risotto. Garnish with extra herbs or a crack of black pepper.
